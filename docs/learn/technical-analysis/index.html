Bollinger Bands Explained: How to Read Squeezes and Breakouts for High-Probability Trades
Bollinger Bands are a volatility indicator that plots three lines: a 20-period moving average (middle band) with upper and lower bands 2 standard deviations away. Squeezes (bands close together) precede breakouts; expansion (bands widening) confirms trend strength.

Breakout vs Fakeout: How to Identify Real Breakouts and Avoid False Breakouts with 70%+ Accuracy
Most breakouts fail. Real breakouts break on 2x+ volume, close above range on first bar, and hold above range on second day. Fakeouts reverse within 1-3 bars on low volume.
